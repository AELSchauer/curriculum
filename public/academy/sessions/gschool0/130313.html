Fruit Stand

#variety

  1. A new apple defaults to ‘Granny Smith’
  2. A new apple can be of another variety

Default slicing behavior

  1. A new apple has 8 #remaining_slices
  2. #slice takes off a single slice by default and gives it to you
  3. When you’ve sliced one off, there are 7 left

Effective slicing behavior

  1. #slice(n) slices off n slices and gives them to you
  2. If you slice 5 slices off your apple, you have 3 #remaining_slices

Greedy slicing behavior

  1. If you slice off more than you have, then you get however many slices are left. 8.a) try: slice(9) off a new apple 8.b) try: slice(3) three times in a row
  2. If you slice off more than you have, then there are no #remaining_slices

#ripe? & #unripe?

  1. Apples are still #unripe? after 9 seconds.
  2. Apples are #ripe? 10 seconds after creating them.

US Phone Number

  1. Anything that is not a digit gets cleaned up. e.g. (123) 456-7890 becomes 1234567890 e.g. 123.456.7890 becomes 1234567890
  2. A number is valid if it is 10 digits.
  3. A number is valid if it is 11 digits and the first is 1.
  4. A valid 11 digit number gets the first digit removed. e.g. 11234567890 becomes 1234567890
  5. An 11 digit number is invalid in all other cases.
  6. A 9 digit number (or shorter) is invalid
  7. A phone number has an #area_code e.g. for 1234567890 the area code is 123
  8. A phone number can be pretty-printed (#to_s) e.g. 1234567890 becomes (123) 456-7890
